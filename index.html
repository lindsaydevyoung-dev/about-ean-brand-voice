EAN Guidelines

About EAN & Brand Voice

The standards, voice, and principles behind every word EAN publishes, built so trust grows as fast as the network.

Mission Statement

To link Donors, Foundations, Non-Profits, and Recipients in one verified philanthropic network, ensuring that every dollar moves with urgency and full visibility from Donor to Recipient, closing the gap between intent and impact.

Vision Statement

A future where philanthropy runs on trust, speed, and transparency, where every organization, contribution, and Recipient is linked in one connected chain, and no disaster survivor waits weeks for help that should have arrived in hours.

Values: The Four Pillars

Pillar 01

Speed

When disaster strikes, every hour matters. Because EAN is already built and Non-Profits are already in the network, there's no setup, no delays. ACH bank transfers and physical cards move money to Recipients the moment it's needed.

Pillar 02

Transparency

Every dollar tracked like a package with a tracking number, every step timestamped. Donors see exactly where contributions go. Foundations get audit trails that boards require. Real-time proof that every dollar reached its destination.

Pillar 03

Credibility

EAN membership signals that your organization meets the standard for verified emergency response. Automatic reporting, secure audit trails, and full compliance documentation are built into every transaction, giving your board, your funders, and your regulators everything they need.

Pillar 04

Integrity

Every chain is only as strong as its final link. In EAN, the final link closes with a thank you, verified proof that real relief reached a real person. Recipients voluntarily rate the Non-Profits and Foundations that served them, and that feedback flows back through the entire chain to the Donors whose generosity made it possible.

Who EAN speaks to, and what we want them to do.

EAN speaks to donors and organizations who are frustrated by the delays and disconnected systems that slow disaster giving down. They are ready to invest in a unified network that lets them respond instantly when disaster strikes, with full visibility into where every dollar goes. The goal is for that audience to see EAN as the network built to close the gap between intent and impact, and to join before the next disaster hits.

Social Media Content Boundaries

Clear lines the work does not cross. These protect Recipients, partners, and the integrity of the network.

Never do this

  • Never use disaster footage or imagery that exploits survivors.
  • Never share Recipient stories or names without verified consent.
  • Avoid politicizing disasters or aligning with partisan messaging.
  • Never speculate on active disaster events for engagement.
  • Avoid corporate jargon, buzzwords, and overly polished language.
  • Never make promises the network cannot back with proof.
  • Avoid trend-chasing content that conflicts with the seriousness of the work.
  • Never reduce Recipients to statistics or stock-photo stand-ins.
  • Avoid fear-based messaging or urgency tactics designed to manipulate.
  • Never disclose partner organization details without explicit approval.
  • Avoid comparing EAN to specific competitors by name.
  • Never publish Donor or contribution amounts without permission.

Social Media Content Preferences

The formats, themes, and creative constraints that define every piece of EAN content.

Preferred Formats

  • Verified Recipient testimonials in their own words.
  • Process explainers that walk through how funds move through the network.
  • Data visualizations showing speed, traceability, and impact.
  • Short-form video featuring real network activations.
  • Partner spotlights highlighting Foundations and Non-Profits in the network.
  • Educational carousels breaking down complex giving concepts.
  • Behind-the-scenes content showing how the network is built and maintained.
  • Quote graphics pulling directly from Recipient feedback.

Recurring Themes

  • The cost of slow disaster relief.
  • What full transparency in philanthropy actually looks like.
  • How verification protects every party in the giving chain.
  • The difference between intent and impact.
  • Why proactive infrastructure matters before disaster strikes.
  • Real Recipient voices and verified outcomes.
  • The role of trust in modern philanthropy.
  • How disconnected systems fail the people they are meant to serve.

Creative Constraints

  • Stay within the EAN color palette and typography system.
  • Never use stock imagery to represent Recipients.
  • Keep visuals clean, structured, and professional.
  • Use plain language at every reading level.
  • Avoid trend-driven formats that undermine the seriousness of the work.
  • Maintain consistent role capitalization (Donor, Foundation, Non-Profit, Recipient).
  • Never use em dashes in copy.
  • Always reinforce the "Trusted Philanthropy. Fully Linked." brand language.

Storytelling Approach

Twelve principles that shape every narrative, from a single quote graphic to a full campaign.

Lead with the human outcome, then show the system that made it possible.

Let Recipients speak for themselves through verified testimonials.

Walk through real activations from disaster trigger to fund disbursement.

Use timestamps and traceable data to show speed and accountability.

Show the gap between intent and impact, then show how EAN closes it.

Highlight Foundation and Non-Profit partners as the heroes of the network.

Break complex philanthropic processes into clear, sequential steps.

Contrast the old way of disaster giving with what the network makes possible.

Use real numbers and verified outcomes instead of generalizations.

Frame every story around one of the four roles in the chain.

Show preparation as the quiet work that makes fast response possible.

Connect every story back to the people on the receiving end.
